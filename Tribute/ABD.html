Abraham Benjamin de Villiers

South African cricketer

Biography

Abram Benjamin De Villiers, born April 15, 1985, in Cape Town, South Africa, is a dedicated advocate for social justice and community empowerment. He holds a degree in Sociology and a Master's in Social Work, specializing in mental health support and community development. Throughout his career, De Villiers has worked with various organizations to address inequality, poverty, and mental health stigma. He is also a passionate environmentalist, actively promoting sustainable practices to combat climate change. Beyond his professional pursuits, De Villiers finds solace in nature, often engaging in outdoor activities like hiking and practicing mindfulness through yoga and meditation. His commitment to both personal well-being and social impact defines his holistic approach to life.

Achievements

  • Fastest ODI Century (2015):Scored 100 runs off just 31 balls against the West Indies on January 18, 2015.
  • Fastest 50 and 150 in ODIs (2015): Scored the fastest fifty (16 balls) and the fastest 150 (64 balls) in the same match against the West Indies.
  • Memorable IPL Performance (2015): Played a match-winning innings of 133* off 59 balls for Royal Challengers Bangalore against Mumbai Indians.
  • First South African to Score a Double Century in Tests Against India (2008): Scored 217* in Ahmedabad.
  • Most ICC ODI Player of the Year Awards (2010, 2014, 2015): Named the ICC ODI Player of the Year three times.
